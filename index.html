Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The "Paraphrase Project" discovered a critical vulnerability where AI-powered tools could "paraphrase" toxic protein sequences, making them undetectable by existing biosecurity screening systems, dubbed the first "zero day" in AI biosecurity. A cross-sector team then developed red-teaming approaches and "patches" to create a more robust and AI-resilient protein screening system, akin to cybersecurity responses. This initiative also established a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" approach for responsible data sharing, highlighting the ongoing necessity for vigilance, collaboration, and proactive policy in managing AI's dual-use risks in biology.

iOS 26.1 Beta 4 Adds Toggle to Turn Off Lock Screen Camera Swipe

The fourth beta of iOS 26.1 includes a new, long-awaited option to disable the Lock Screen swipe that activates the camera. This feature addresses a common user complaint about accidental camera activation and potential misuse for unauthorized photos. iPhone users can now turn off the "Lock Screen Swipe to Open Camera" setting within the Camera section of the Settings app.
