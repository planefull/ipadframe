Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but create privacy risks through "linkability," allowing separate uses of credentials to be tracked and associated with individuals. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that introduces "unlinkability" to common digital identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent uses zero-knowledge proofs to enable users to selectively disclose information and prevent their separate digital interactions from being linked, all without requiring existing credential issuance systems to be updated.
