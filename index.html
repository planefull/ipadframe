Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, pose significant privacy risks due to "linkability," where hidden identifiers allow tracking of user activities across various credential uses. To counter this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to common digital identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge proofs and a two-stage process to enable users to prove specific facts without revealing any linking information, effectively enhancing privacy without requiring extensive changes to existing infrastructure.
