Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, introduce privacy risks like "linkability," allowing separate uses of credentials to be tracked and linked to a specific person for profiling purposes. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to common identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent leverages efficient zero-knowledge proofs to enable users to selectively disclose information without revealing linking identifiers, all without requiring credential issuers to update their existing systems.

Foldable iPhone Under-Screen Touch ID Rumor Dismissed by Analyst

Apple analyst Ming-Chi Kuo is standing by his prediction that Apple's first foldable iPhone, anticipated for 2026, will feature Touch ID integrated into the side button rather than an under-display sensor. He dismissed recent market rumors of an under-display ultrasonic fingerprint sensor, noting that a side-button design saves internal space by skipping Face ID, a method already used in some iPads. Kuo's forecast details a book-style foldable device with a 7.8-inch inner display, 5.5-inch outer screen, dual-lens camera, and an estimated price between $2,000 and $2,500.
