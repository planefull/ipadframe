Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers in credentials like mobile driver's licenses or app logins can be used to track and profile user activities across separate interactions. To address this, Microsoft Research has launched Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used digital identity formats, including JSON Web Tokens. Crescent leverages zero-knowledge proofs to enable users to verify specific facts about their credentials without revealing any underlying data or identifiers that could link their separate digital interactions, thereby enhancing privacy without requiring changes to existing issuing systems.

Apple Seeds Ninth watchOS 26, tvOS 26, and visionOS 26 Developer Betas

Apple has released the ninth developer betas for watchOS 26, tvOS 26, and visionOS 26, with watchOS and tvOS also available to public testers. watchOS 26 introduces an AI Workout Buddy, a Notes app, and Live Translation, while tvOS 26 enables iPhone karaoke and improves AirPlay speaker integration, with both adopting a new Liquid Glass design. VisionOS 26 adds support for spatial widgets and shared spatial experiences, and all these updates are anticipated to launch officially this fall.
