Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but create privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers allow tracking of separate credential uses to build user profiles. To combat this, Microsoft Research introduced Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Using zero-knowledge proofs, Crescent allows users to selectively disclose information and prove facts about their credentials without revealing underlying data or identifiers that could link their digital interactions, all without requiring updates from credential issuers.

iPhone 17 Air to Start at $1,099 With 256GB Storage, 1TB for $1,499

According to a TrendForce report, Apple's new iPhone 17 Air will start at $1,099 with 256GB base storage and replace the iPhone 16 Plus. The iPhone 17 Pro and Pro Max models are also expected to increase their base storage to 256GB and see price increases of $50-$100, while the standard iPhone 17 will maintain its $799 starting price and 128GB base. Additionally, all iPhone 17 models are anticipated to feature 120Hz ProMotion displays, with the Air, Pro, and Pro Max gaining 12GB of RAM and new A19/A19 Pro chips.
