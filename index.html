Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project revealed a "zero day" vulnerability where AI protein design tools could reformulate toxic proteins, like ricin, to evade existing biosecurity screening systems. In response, a cross-sector team developed and deployed "patches" that strengthened global nucleic acid synthesis screening capabilities to detect these AI-generated variants. This effort also established a novel, perpetually managed disclosure process for sensitive dual-use research, underscoring the ongoing need for vigilance and collaboration in managing AI's impact on biosecurity.
