Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Genetic professionals face significant challenges in diagnosing rare diseases due to the overwhelming volume and complexity of whole genome sequencing data, leading to low initial diagnosis rates and prolonged patient suffering. To address this, Microsoft Research, Drexel University, and the Broad Institute developed a generative AI-based assistant to enhance the "sensemaking" tasks inherent in genetic analysis. This AI tool aims to streamline workflows by flagging cases for reanalysis based on new scientific findings and synthesizing crucial gene and variant information, ultimately improving diagnostic yield and reducing the time to diagnosis for rare genetic conditions.

Apple Releases Safari Technology Preview 229 With Bug Fixes and Performance Improvements

Apple has released Safari Technology Preview 229, an update to its experimental browser designed for users to test features planned for future Safari releases and provide feedback. This latest version includes numerous fixes and updates across various browser components, such as Accessibility, CSS, JavaScript, and Web API. The update is compatible with macOS Sequoia and Tahoe and is available for anyone to download and use without a developer account.
