Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers can track users across various credential uses. To address this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds unlinkability to widely used identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent leverages zero-knowledge proofs to allow users to selectively present information without revealing underlying data or linking separate interactions, crucially without requiring issuers to update their systems.

Refurbished Apple Pencil Pro Now Available in U.S. at a Discount

The Apple Pencil Pro is now available for the first time in Apple's online refurbished stores in the U.S. and Canada, following its May 2024 launch. Priced at $109 in the U.S., a roughly 15% discount, this refurbished option is currently less compelling as a new Apple Pencil Pro can be purchased for only $1.87 more on Amazon. While Apple's refurbished products are meticulously tested and cleaned, offering like-new condition, the minimal savings make it a questionable choice compared to buying new.
