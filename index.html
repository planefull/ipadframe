Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The "Paraphrase Project," co-led by Microsoft's Chief Scientific Officer Eric Horvitz, uncovered a "zero-day" vulnerability where AI-powered protein design tools could reformulate toxic proteins to evade existing biosecurity screening systems. In response, a cross-sector team developed and deployed more robust AI-resilient screening solutions, akin to cybersecurity patches, which were shared globally through the International Gene Synthesis Consortium (IGSC). This collaborative effort also pioneered a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" strategy for managing access to sensitive dual-use research data, aiming to balance open science with minimizing information hazards for future advancements in biosecurity.

Samsung to Unveil Apple Vision Pro Competitor Tomorrow

Samsung will unveil its long-awaited "Project Moohan" mixed reality headset tomorrow at a Galaxy Event, positioning it as a direct competitor to the Apple Vision Pro. This "AI-native" device will run Android XR, an operating system co-created with Google and Qualcomm, offering virtual immersion and real-world passthrough capabilities. Described as lightweight and ergonomically optimized, the headset will boast enhanced AI features via Gemini and integrate various Google services for a comprehensive user experience.
