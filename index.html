Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while offering convenience, pose significant privacy risks through "linkability," allowing separate uses of credentials to be tracked and associated with a specific person. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent employs zero-knowledge proofs and a two-stage process to enable users to selectively disclose information without revealing underlying data or linking their interactions, all without requiring credential issuers to update their existing systems.
