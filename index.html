Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The Model Context Protocol (MCP) is fostering a "society of agents" by enabling horizontal integration of diverse AI tools, moving beyond vertically integrated systems. However, this new paradigm introduces "tool-space interference," where challenges like overwhelming tool counts, excessively long responses, complex parameter schemas, naming conflicts, and inadequate error messages reduce agent effectiveness. To address these issues, the article recommends implementing formal namespaces, enhancing resource sharing protocols, encouraging transparent server documentation, developing client-side optimizations, and leveraging marketplaces for compatibility to build a more robust and efficient AI agent ecosystem.

'A New Story': Apple Store in Tokyo Returning to Original 2003 Location

Apple Ginza is returning to its original location in Tokyo's famous shopping district after operating from a temporary spot for over three years. The temporary store will permanently close on Monday, September 22, with the new store at the rebuilt original site expected to open in the second half of 2025. To celebrate the reopening of what was Apple's first retail store outside the U.S., a special commemorative wallpaper has been released.
