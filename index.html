Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, pose privacy risks due to "linkability," where hidden identifiers allow tracking and profiling of users' separate credential uses. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that introduces "unlinkability" to widely used digital identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge proofs to enable users to selectively disclose information without revealing any underlying data that could link their interactions, and it works without requiring credential issuers to update their systems.
