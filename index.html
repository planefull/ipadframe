Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, such as those in phone wallets and app logins, offer convenience but introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where separate uses of a credential can be tracked to build user profiles. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driverâ€™s licenses. Crescent achieves this by using efficient zero-knowledge proofs, allowing users to selectively disclose information without revealing identifiers that could link their separate digital interactions, and importantly, it works without requiring credential issuers to update their systems.

iOS 26: Silence Wallet App Promo and Offer Notifications

After widespread complaints about unwanted promotional alerts, Apple has introduced a solution in iOS 26 to silence marketing messages from the Wallet app. Users can now find a dedicated "Offers & Promotions" toggle within the Wallet app's notification settings to disable these alerts. This allows iPhone owners to stop receiving promotional content while ensuring important transaction and payment notifications remain active.
