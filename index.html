Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Microsoft Research Podcast features the "Paraphrase Project," revealing how AI tools can redesign toxic proteins to evade existing biosecurity screening systems, creating a "zero day" vulnerability. A collaborative team from Microsoft, Twist Bioscience, and IBBIS developed a "red-teaming" approach to expose these weaknesses and then implemented "patches" to create more robust, AI-resilient protein screening systems, which were shared globally. This initiative underscores the necessity for continuous vigilance, proactive defense, and innovative strategies for responsible information sharing to navigate the dual-use challenges of AI in synthetic biology.

Apple's Health and Fitness Teams Report to Eddy Cue in Reshuffle

Apple is reorganizing its health and fitness divisions, placing them under its Services group led by Eddy Cue, following the impending departure of COO Jeff Williams. This restructuring will see health lead Sumbul Desai consolidate the teams, with Fitness+ head Jay Blahnik reporting to her, as the company prepares to launch a new "Health+" subscription service with an AI assistant next year. The move aims to transform Apple's wellness initiatives into a more significant revenue stream, while other leadership changes include Craig Federighi adding watchOS to his oversight and John Ternus taking sole control of Apple Watch hardware.
