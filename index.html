Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, pose significant privacy risks through "linkability," allowing various uses of a person's digital credentials to be tracked and profiled. To counter this, Microsoft Research has introduced Crescent, a cryptographic library that brings "unlinkability" to common identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes efficient zero-knowledge proofs, optimized for mobile devices, to ensure users can selectively disclose necessary information without revealing identifiers or allowing separate digital interactions to be connected, all without requiring updates from credential-issuing organizations.
