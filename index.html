Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, despite their convenience, pose significant privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers allow tracking and profiling of users across different credential uses. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent achieves this by employing efficient zero-knowledge proofs, allowing users to selectively disclose information and prove facts about their credentials without revealing linking identifiers or requiring updates to existing issuer systems.

iOS 26: Capture iPhone Screen Content in HDR

Apple's iOS 26 update introduces an optional HDR (High Dynamic Range) screen capture feature, enhancing the quality of screenshots and video recordings by preserving rich colors and intricate details. This new capability is particularly useful for capturing high-contrast content and documenting app interfaces with greater fidelity, although it results in larger file sizes. Users can enable HDR capture through the Screen Capture settings, and iOS 26 also adds other options like full-screen previews, automatic visual look up, and CarPlay screenshot support.
