RenderFormer: How neural networks are reshaping 3D rendering

Microsoft Research has developed RenderFormer, a novel neural architecture that fully replaces traditional 3D rendering with machine learning, eliminating the need for physics-based techniques like ray tracing or rasterization. This model leverages triangle tokens for scene geometry and materials, and ray bundle tokens for viewing directions, processing them through dual-branch transformers to handle both view-independent and view-dependent rendering effects. RenderFormer represents a significant breakthrough for neural rendering, demonstrating deep learning's capability to learn a complete graphics pipeline, support arbitrary 3D inputs with global illumination, and serve as a foundation for future AI integrations in visual computing.
