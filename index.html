Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Diagnosing rare genetic diseases is challenging due to the vast, complex data from whole genome sequencing, leading to low initial diagnosis rates and difficult reanalysis. To address this, Microsoft Research, Drexel University, and the Broad Institute collaborated to design a generative AI assistant that enhances genetic professionals' "sensemaking" workflows. This AI tool aims to streamline diagnosis by automatically flagging cases for reanalysis based on new scientific findings and synthesizing critical gene and variant data, ultimately improving diagnostic yield and patient outcomes.
