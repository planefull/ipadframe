Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce significant privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers connect separate uses of credentials, enabling detailed user tracking. Existing privacy features like selective disclosure often fall short, as serial numbers or cryptographic signatures can still link interactions. To combat this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that uses zero-knowledge proofs to add "unlinkability" to common digital identity formats, allowing users to prove facts without revealing any data that could link their activities across different services.
