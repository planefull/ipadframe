Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers can track users across various credential uses. To counter this, Microsoft Research has developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent employs zero-knowledge proofs to allow users to selectively disclose information without revealing underlying data or identifiers that could link their separate digital interactions, all without requiring changes to existing issuer systems.

AirPods Pro 3 Won't Get 'Major' Audio or Active Noise Cancellation Improvements

According to Mark Gurman, the upcoming AirPods Pro 3 will not feature significant improvements to audio quality or Active Noise Cancellation. Instead, the primary new feature will be heart rate tracking, utilizing LED optical sensors. Additionally, the new earbuds will boast an updated design for a better fit and a slimmer, smaller charging case.
