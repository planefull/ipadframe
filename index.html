Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers allow separate uses of credentials (such as mobile driver's licenses or app logins) to be connected, enabling extensive user tracking. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats, including JSON Web Tokens. Crescent employs efficient zero-knowledge proofs, allowing users to selectively disclose information without revealing any data that could link their separate digital interactions, all without requiring credential issuers to update their existing systems.

Apple Hints at iPhone 17 Models Lacking SIM Card Slot in More Countries

Apple is hinting at plans to eliminate physical SIM card trays from iPhones in more countries, starting with the upcoming iPhone 17 series. This is strongly suggested by mandatory eSIM training for retail employees in the EU, due just before the iPhone 17 announcement. Following the US, where iPhone 14 models and newer are eSIM-only, this international shift offers enhanced security and convenience for travelers by removing physical SIMs.
