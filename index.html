Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, pose significant privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers allow user activities to be tracked and profiled across various applications. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes efficient zero-knowledge proofs and a two-stage process, enhancing privacy by preventing separate digital interactions from being linked, all without requiring credential issuers to update their systems.

Apple Designed Interactive Logo for September 9 Event

Apple has created a unique, interactive logo for its upcoming September 9 "Awe Dropping" event, prominently displayed on its main website. This animated logo changes colors from blue to orange when touched or swiped on various devices, resembling a thermal map. The shifting colors are believed to be a subtle hint towards anticipated new iPhone colors, including orange, dark blue, and light blue, for the upcoming iPhone 17 Pro and Air models.
