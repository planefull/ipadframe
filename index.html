Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers can track user activities across various digital credential uses, such as mobile driver's licenses or app logins. To counter this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to common identity formats like JSON Web Tokens, without requiring issuers to update their systems. Crescent achieves this by using efficient zero-knowledge proofs, allowing users to selectively disclose information and preventing any part of the credential from linking separate interactions beyond what is explicitly revealed.

Garmin Beats Apple to Market with Satellite-Connected Smartwatch

Garmin has launched its Fenix 8 Pro smartwatch, featuring inReach satellite and cellular connectivity, just days before Apple is expected to unveil a similar Apple Watch Ultra model. This new device allows users to send text messages, share location, and utilize an SOS emergency feature via satellite, along with standard cellular capabilities. The Fenix 8 Pro, which includes a high-resolution microLED display and advanced health tracking, starts at $1,200 and requires a separate monthly subscription for its satellite services, unlike Apple's current free satellite offering.
