Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project, a collaborative effort led by Microsoft Research, revealed that AI-powered tools can redesign toxic protein sequences in ways that evade existing biosecurity screening systems, posing a "zero-day" vulnerability. To address this, a cross-sector team developed more robust screening methods and "patches" that were then shared with global DNA synthesis companies to strengthen defenses against AI-enabled misuse. The project also pioneered a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" strategy for responsibly sharing sensitive research data, aiming to balance open science with minimizing information hazards in dual-use technologies.
