Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers connect separate online activities to a user, creating detailed profiles. To address this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to common digital identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent uses efficient zero-knowledge proofs to allow users to selectively disclose information and prove facts without revealing identifying data or linking distinct interactions, and it works without requiring credential issuers to update their systems.
