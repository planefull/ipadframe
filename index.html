Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, despite their convenience, introduce privacy risks through "linkability," allowing separate uses of credentials (like mobile driver's licenses or app logins) to be tracked and profiled via hidden identifiers. To address this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to existing identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent achieves this by using efficient zero-knowledge proofs, enabling users to prove specific facts about their credentials without revealing linking data or requiring issuing organizations to update their systems.
