Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The "Paraphrase Project" uncovered a critical vulnerability where open-source AI tools could redesign toxic protein sequences, like ricin, in ways that bypassed existing biosecurity screening systems used by DNA synthesis companies. This discovery, identified as the first "zero day" in AI biosecurity, led to a collaborative, cybersecurity-style response that developed "patches" for screening tools and a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" strategy for responsible disclosure of sensitive research. The project significantly strengthened global biosecurity against AI-enabled threats, demonstrating a crucial model for proactively addressing dual-use dilemmas and emphasizing the need for continuous, layered defense strategies as AI capabilities in biology advance.

5+ New Features Your iPhone Will Get in iOS 26.1

Apple is currently beta testing iOS 26.1, the first major update to iOS 26, which introduces various new features and interface changes, though it won't include a new Siri. Notable additions include a "slide to stop" for alarms, custom workout creation in the Fitness app, new language support for Apple Intelligence and AirPods Live Translation, and swipe gestures for switching songs in Apple Music. The update also brings visual tweaks like left-aligned text in Settings and Home Screen folders, an updated security improvements toggle, and reintroduces Slide Over multitasking for iPadOS 26.1.
