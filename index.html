Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The "Paraphrase Project" revealed a critical "zero-day" vulnerability where AI tools could redesign toxic proteins to evade existing biosecurity screening systems, prompting concerns about dual-use technology risks. To address this, a collaborative team developed an AI red-teaming pipeline to generate these AI-rewritten proteins, subsequently designing and implementing "patches" to create more robust screening tools. This effort significantly strengthened global biosecurity measures, was published in *Science*, and established a new framework for the responsible disclosure and sharing of sensitive dual-use research data.

Apple's 2026 Foldable iPhone Rumored to Use Titanium and Aluminum Frame

Apple's upcoming foldable iPhone, expected to launch in 2026 as part of the iPhone 18 lineup, is rumored to feature a frame made from a mix of titanium and aluminum. This choice of materials is crucial for ensuring the device's durability and preventing bending, especially for its anticipated ultra-thin design.
