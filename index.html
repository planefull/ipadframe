Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The "Paraphrase Project," led by Microsoft Chief Scientific Officer Eric Horvitz, uncovered a critical "zero day" vulnerability where AI tools could redesign toxic proteins to bypass existing biosecurity screening systems used by DNA synthesis companies. A cross-sector team then collaborated to develop an AI biosecurity red-teaming pipeline, successfully demonstrating how AI-generated protein variants could evade screening and subsequently patching these systems to make them more robust. This effort not only strengthened global biosecurity screening but also established a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" approach for responsible data and software sharing, addressing the dual-use dilemma of emerging AI technologies.
