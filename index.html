Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The Model Context Protocol (MCP) is enabling a "society of tools" and horizontal integration for AI agents, moving beyond traditional vertically integrated systems. However, this ecosystem is encountering "tool-space interference," where factors like excessive tool counts, lengthy responses, complex parameters, naming collisions, and inadequate error messages reduce overall agent effectiveness. To address these challenges, the article proposes recommendations across protocol, server, client, and marketplace developers, including formal namespaces, better resource sharing, transparent documentation, and client-side optimizations to ensure compatibility and scalability.

iPhone 17: Using the New Center Stage Front Camera

Apple's new iPhone 17 and iPhone Air models feature an 18MP Center Stage front camera with a unique square sensor and intelligent auto-adjustment capabilities. This system automatically rotates and zooms the camera's field of view to perfectly frame subjects, expanding for groups and maintaining focus without the user needing to physically move the phone. Users can easily enable, disable, or manually override the auto-rotate and auto-zoom functions for greater control over their shots.
