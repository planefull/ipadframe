Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

As AI agents increasingly rely on diverse tools and other agents via the Model Context Protocol (MCP), a new challenge called "tool-space interference" emerges, hindering overall effectiveness. This interference stems from issues like excessive tool counts, overly long responses, complex parameter schemas, naming conflicts, and poor error handling within the rapidly expanding MCP ecosystem. To address these problems, the article recommends collaboration among protocol, server, client, and market developers to implement solutions such as formal namespaces, improved documentation, and client-side optimization strategies to ensure a scalable and interoperable agent infrastructure.

iFixit Teardown Reveals How Apple Made the iPhone Air So Thin

iFixit's teardown of the new iPhone Air, Apple's thinnest iPhone to date, revealed how its 5.6mm profile was achieved through design innovations like a camera "plateau" housing part of the logic board and a large, metal-encased battery. The titanium frame, while generally bend-proof, has weak points due to plastic gaps for cellular interference. Surprisingly, despite its slim design, the iPhone Air earned a provisional repairability score of 7 out of 10, thanks to accessible components like the battery and display, and Apple's improved repair policies.
