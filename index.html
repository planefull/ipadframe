Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks through "linkability," allowing hidden identifiers to track user activities and build profiles across various digital interactions. To address this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to popular identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes optimized zero-knowledge proofs to enable users to selectively disclose information and prevent their separate digital uses from being connected, all without requiring existing credential-issuing organizations to update their systems.
