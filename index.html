Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The rapid growth of agentic AI and the Model Context Protocol (MCP) is enabling a "society of agents" and "tools" through horizontal integration, which expands capabilities but also introduces new challenges. This proliferation leads to "tool-space interference," where diverse tools can cause issues like excessive counts, long response lengths, complex parameters, and naming conflicts, hindering overall agent effectiveness. To address this, the article recommends protocol enhancements such as formal namespaces and better client-provided resource handling, alongside best practices for server, client, and marketplace developers to improve compatibility, usability, and optimization within the ecosystem.
