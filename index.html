Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project, co-led by Microsoft Chief Scientific Officer Eric Horvitz, uncovered a "zero day" vulnerability where AI-powered protein design tools could reformulate toxic protein sequences to evade existing biosecurity screening systems. A cross-sector team then developed an AI "red-teaming" pipeline to demonstrate this risk and subsequently created and shared "patches" to strengthen these screening systems globally. This effort not only significantly improved AI-resilient biosecurity but also established a new "perpetual gatekeeping" strategy for responsible disclosure of dual-use scientific information, highlighting the ongoing need for vigilance and adaptive governance in the age of AI.
