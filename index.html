Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while offering convenience, pose privacy risks through "linkability," allowing separate uses of credentials to be tracked and associated with individuals. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that introduces "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent leverages zero-knowledge proofs to enable users to selectively disclose information and prevent tracking across interactions, performing complex computations efficiently on mobile devices and without requiring system updates from credential issuers.
