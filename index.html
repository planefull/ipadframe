Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but pose significant privacy risks due to "linkability," where hidden identifiers allow tracking and profiling by connecting separate uses of credentials like mobile driver's licenses and app logins. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that introduces "unlinkability" to common identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge proofs to enable users to selectively share necessary information without revealing linking identifiers, protecting privacy across digital interactions efficiently and without requiring system updates from credential issuers.
