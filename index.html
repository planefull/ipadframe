Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers in credentials enable tracking and profiling of user behavior across various applications and services. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used digital identity formats, including JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge proofs to allow users to selectively disclose information and prove facts about their credentials without revealing underlying data or linking separate interactions, thereby protecting privacy without requiring infrastructure changes from credential issuers.

Five Ways iOS 26 Improves Your iPhone's Battery

iOS 26 features a redesigned Battery section, providing users with a more in-depth weekly view of their usage, comparisons to average consumption, and detailed insights into app-specific battery drain. A new "Adaptive Power Mode" intelligently adjusts performance to extend battery life during high usage, available on compatible devices. Additional enhancements include visible charging time estimates on the lock screen, a Dynamic Island prompt for Low Power Mode, and more descriptive battery icons.
