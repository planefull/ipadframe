Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, pose significant privacy risks due to "linkability," which allows various uses of a person's digital credentials to be associated and tracked. To address this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that introduces "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent uses efficient zero-knowledge proofs to let users selectively share information without revealing underlying data or linking identifiers, and crucially, it works without requiring issuing organizations to update their existing systems.

Apple Slashes 2025 MLS Season Pass to as Low as $25

Apple has significantly discounted the MLS Season Pass for the remainder of the 2025 season, now offering it for $29, or $25 for Apple TV+ subscribers, a sharp reduction from its regular $99 price. This reduced annual subscription includes access to every live and on-demand match, playoffs, and features like "MLS 360" and "Sunday Night Soccer." While monthly subscriptions remain unchanged, the discounted annual pass will automatically renew at the standard $99 price for the 2026 season unless canceled manually.
