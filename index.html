Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project uncovered a "zero-day" vulnerability where AI tools could redesign toxic protein sequences to bypass existing biosecurity screening systems, posing a significant dual-use risk. A collaborative cross-sector team used an AI "red-teaming" pipeline to generate thousands of evasive variants, which enabled the development and global dissemination of more robust protein screening solutions. This initiative also established a novel perpetual gatekeeping strategy for responsible data sharing, highlighting the critical need for continuous vigilance, proactive defense, and international cooperation in managing AI-enabled biosecurity threats.

Facebook's New Collage Feature May Use Your Images for AI Training

Meta is rolling out a new opt-in AI-based collage and photo editing tool for Facebook users in the U.S. and Canada, which scans camera rolls to suggest "fun collages and edits." This feature uploads your images to the cloud for AI processing and suggests private edits, recaps, and themed collages that can be shared in Stories and Feed. Users should be aware that sharing content created by this AI will use their images for AI training, aligning with Meta's broader strategy of using user data to improve its AI tools.
