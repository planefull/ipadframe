Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project, led by Microsoft Chief Scientific Officer Eric Horvitz and a cross-sector team, uncovered a "zero-day" vulnerability where AI tools could redesign toxic protein sequences, like ricin, to bypass existing biosecurity screening systems. In response, the team developed an AI biosecurity "red-teaming" approach to identify such vulnerabilities and then created and shared "patches"—more robust protein screening systems—with DNA synthesis companies worldwide. This collaborative effort, detailed in *Science*, established a new model for managing information hazards through perpetual gatekeeping and demonstrated a proactive approach to strengthening global biosecurity against the dual-use risks of advancing AI capabilities in synthetic biology.
