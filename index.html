Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The "Paraphrase Project," led by Microsoft Chief Scientific Officer Eric Horvitz and a cross-sector team, uncovered a critical vulnerability where AI-powered protein design tools could reformulate toxic proteins to bypass existing biosecurity screening systems. This "zero day" finding led to a cybersecurity-style response, developing and implementing more robust protein screening systems now shared globally to strengthen defenses. The project also introduced a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" strategy for responsible data and software sharing, aiming to balance open science with minimizing information hazards in the evolving landscape of AI and biosecurity.
