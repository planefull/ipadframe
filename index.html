Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, introduce privacy risks through "linkability," allowing separate uses of credentials to be tracked and associated with individuals. Microsoft Research has developed Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Using zero-knowledge proofs, Crescent allows users to prove specific facts about their credentials without revealing underlying data or linking their separate digital interactions, all without requiring credential issuers to update their existing systems.

Refurbished Apple Pencil Pro Now Available in U.S. at a Discount

The Apple Pencil Pro is now available for the first time in Apple's online refurbished stores in the U.S. and Canada, following its launch in May 2024. Priced at $109 in the U.S., it offers a 15% discount from its new price of $129, though Amazon currently sells new units for just slightly more. Despite Apple's rigorous refurbishment process ensuring like-new condition, the minimal savings make the refurbished option less compelling for some buyers.
