Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce significant privacy risks due to "linkability," where separate uses of credentials can be tracked and profiled. To counter this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to common identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge proofs, allowing users to selectively disclose information without revealing underlying data or identifiers that could link their interactions, and importantly, it works without requiring credential issuers to update their systems.

Astropad's 'Fresh Coat' Screen Protector Cuts iPhone Reflections by 75% Without Color Loss

Astropad has launched "Fresh Coat," a new anti-reflective glass screen protector designed to make iPhones easier to see outdoors and under bright lights. It significantly reduces reflections by up to 75% while preserving color accuracy and display visibility, utilizing a five-layer system for protection and an included tray for foolproof installation. Fresh Coat is available now for $27.99 for various iPhone models, including the iPhone 15 Pro, 16 Pro, and 17, and is designed to preserve Apple's own anti-reflective coating.
