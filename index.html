Breaking the networking wall in AI infrastructure 

AI system performance is increasingly limited by network and memory bottlenecks, driven by a fundamental trade-off between the short reach of power-efficient copper links and the high power consumption and lower reliability of longer-reach optical fibers. To address this, Microsoft developed MOSAIC, a novel optical link technology using a "wide-and-slow" architecture with hundreds of parallel low-speed microLED channels. This approach delivers low power, high reliability, and long reach (up to 50 meters) simultaneously, promising significant energy savings, reduced failure rates, and the ability to unlock more scalable and efficient AI infrastructure designs.
