Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The rise of agentic AI and the Model Context Protocol (MCP) is fostering a "society of agents" and tools, moving beyond vertically integrated systems to enable horizontal integration. However, this shift introduces "tool-space interference," where diverse tools can hinder overall effectiveness due to issues like excessive tool counts, lengthy responses, complex parameters, naming conflicts, and poor error messages. To mitigate these challenges, the article recommends implementing formal namespaces, improving resource sharing specifications, encouraging transparent server documentation, and leveraging client-side optimizations and marketplace best practices for a more compatible and robust AI ecosystem.
