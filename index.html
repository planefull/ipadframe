Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The rise of agentic AI and the Model Context Protocol (MCP) are leading to a "society of agents" that increasingly rely on horizontally integrated, diverse tools, moving beyond vertically integrated systems. This expansion introduces "tool-space interference," where challenges like excessive tool counts, overly long responses, complex parameter schemas, naming conflicts, and poor error messages reduce overall agent effectiveness and lead to task failures. To address this, the article recommends formal namespaces, enhanced protocol support for client-provided resources, transparent server documentation, and market-level optimizations to ensure better compatibility and efficiency across the AI agent ecosystem.
