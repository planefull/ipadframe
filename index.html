Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The "Paraphrase Project," led by Microsoft Chief Scientific Officer Eric Horvitz and a cross-sector team, uncovered a critical "zero day" vulnerability: AI tools could redesign toxic proteins to bypass existing biosecurity screening systems. The team developed a "red-teaming" approach to generate AI-paraphrased toxic protein variants, then designed and implemented robust "patches" for screening software, which have since been shared worldwide to strengthen biosecurity. This initiative established a new model for addressing AI-driven dual-use dilemmas, combining technical solutions with novel transparency and data-sharing protocols to proactively manage emerging biological risks.
