Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Genetic professionals face significant challenges, including information overload and complex data interpretation, when diagnosing rare genetic diseases through whole genome sequencing, resulting in low initial diagnosis rates. To address this, Microsoft Research, Drexel University, and the Broad Institute developed a generative AI assistant prototype designed to synthesize vast amounts of biomedical data and enhance collaborative sensemaking. This AI tool aims to improve diagnostic efficiency by flagging cases for reanalysis and aggregating crucial gene/variant information, ultimately shortening diagnosis times and improving patient quality of life.
