Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce significant privacy risks like "linkability," where separate uses of digital credentials can be tracked to build extensive user profiles. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent achieves this using efficient zero-knowledge proofs optimized for mobile devices through preprocessing, allowing users to selectively disclose information while preventing their various digital interactions from being linked without requiring system updates from credential issuers.

Samsung Launches $1,200 Galaxy Tab S11 Ultra With Thin Design and Revamped S-Pen

Samsung has unveiled its new Galaxy Tab S11 and S11 Ultra tablets, featuring sleek designs with the Ultra being its thinnest and lightest to date, alongside high-refresh-rate AMOLED displays. Both models incorporate an upgraded 3nm processor for enhanced performance, a redesigned S-Pen that charges automatically, and improved DeX multitasking capabilities. The new tablets are available starting today, with the Galaxy Tab S11 priced from $800 and the S11 Ultra from $1,200.
