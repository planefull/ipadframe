Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project, led by Microsoft Chief Scientific Officer Eric Horvitz and a cross-sector team, uncovered a critical vulnerability where open-source AI tools could redesign toxic protein sequences, allowing them to bypass existing biosecurity screening systems. This discovery, deemed the first "zero day" for AI and biosecurity, prompted a collaborative, cybersecurity-style response to develop and deploy more robust screening patches with synthesis companies like Twist Bioscience. Beyond technical fixes, the project also pioneered a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" approach for responsible sharing of sensitive research data, setting a precedent for handling information hazards in dual-use AI and biotechnology fields.

iOS 26 Liquid Glass Design Copied by Android Smartphone Maker

Chinese smartphone maker Vivo has unveiled its new OriginOS 6, which significantly draws inspiration from Apple's "Liquid Glass" design language found in iOS 26. The operating system features a strikingly similar aesthetic, including rounded, translucent elements like buttons, icons, app folders, and notifications, creating an interface that could easily be mistaken for Apple's. Despite Vivo's marketing referencing a "flowing" user experience, the clear visual mimicry of Apple's distinctive design is evident throughout.
