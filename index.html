Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Genetic professionals face significant challenges in diagnosing rare genetic diseases due to the vast and complex data involved in whole genome sequencing, leading to low initial diagnosis rates and time-consuming reanalysis. To address this, Microsoft Research, Drexel University, and the Broad Institute co-designed a generative AI assistant aimed at improving "sensemaking" by synthesizing biomedical data and supporting tasks like flagging cases for reanalysis. This AI-enhanced approach seeks to increase diagnostic yield, shorten the time to diagnosis, and ultimately improve outcomes for patients with rare genetic conditions.
