Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, pose significant privacy risks due to "linkability," where hidden identifiers allow separate uses of credentials like mobile driver's licenses or app logins to be associated and tracked. To address this, Microsoft Research has developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge proofs, optimized for mobile devices, to enable users to present information and prove facts about their credentials without revealing linking data or requiring system updates, thus protecting privacy by preventing the connection of separate digital interactions.
