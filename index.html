Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, create privacy risks through "linkability," allowing separate uses of credentials to be tracked and linked to an individual. To combat this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used digital identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent leverages zero-knowledge proofs, optimized for mobile devices, enabling users to selectively disclose information and prove facts about their credentials without revealing identifiers that could link their separate digital interactions.
