Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The rapid growth of agentic AI and the Model Context Protocol (MCP) is fostering a "society of agents" and "society of tools," enabling diverse AI systems to access vast catalogs of functionalities. However, this horizontal integration introduces "tool-space interference," causing reduced effectiveness, higher costs, and task failures due to issues like excessive tool counts, lengthy responses, complex parameters, and naming collisions. To mitigate these problems, the article recommends protocol enhancements like formal namespaces and client-provided resources, improved server documentation, client-side optimizations, and marketplace best practices to ensure a more robust and interoperable agent ecosystem.
