Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers can connect separate credential uses to track user behavior. To counter this, Microsoft Research has developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used digital identity formats, including JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses, without requiring issuers to update their systems. Crescent achieves this by using zero-knowledge proofs, which enable users to selectively disclose information and prove facts about their credentials without revealing underlying data or allowing their interactions to be linked.
