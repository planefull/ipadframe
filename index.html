Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, introduce privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers can track and associate a user's various digital activities. To combat this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to common identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge proofs and preprocessing to enable users to verify specific information about their credentials without revealing unnecessary data or linking separate interactions, all without requiring updates from credential issuers.

Next Vehicle With CarPlay Ultra Named in Report as Rollout Continues

Hyundai's upcoming IONIQ 3 electric vehicle is reportedly set to feature Apple's advanced CarPlay Ultra system, according to *Top Gear*. Described as a smaller, more affordable EV, the IONIQ 3 will offer up to 365 miles of range and a digital-forward interface, with a concept version debuting in September ahead of a 2026 launch.
