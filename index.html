Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Genetic professionals face significant challenges in diagnosing rare genetic diseases due to the vast and complex data from whole genome sequencing, leading to low initial diagnosis rates and lengthy waits. A collaborative study by Microsoft Research, Drexel University, and the Broad Institute explored designing a generative AI assistant to support these intensive "sensemaking" tasks. This AI tool aims to streamline workflows by flagging cases for reanalysis based on new scientific findings and efficiently synthesizing gene and variant information, ultimately improving diagnostic speed and patient outcomes.
