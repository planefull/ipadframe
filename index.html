Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The "Paraphrase Project," led by Microsoft Chief Scientific Officer Eric Horvitz, uncovered a critical "zero-day" vulnerability where AI tools could redesign toxic protein sequences, enabling them to bypass existing biosecurity screening systems. A cross-sector team addressed this by developing a "red-teaming" pipeline to generate and test these AI-created variants, resulting in a more robust protein screening system and patches shared globally with synthesis companies. This collaborative effort established a model for proactively tackling dual-use AI risks, including a novel approach to responsible information sharing, emphasizing the ongoing need for vigilance in biosecurity as AI capabilities advance.

Apple Rumored to Launch MacBook Pro With M5 Chip Before M5 Pro and M5 Max Models

Apple is reportedly planning a staggered release for its M5 MacBook Pro lineup, with a base model featuring the standard M5 chip expected to launch soon. This initial release will precede the higher-end 14-inch and 16-inch MacBook Pro models, which are anticipated to debut in early 2026 with more powerful M5 Pro and M5 Max chips. This strategy marks a return to a decoupled launch, supported by historical precedents and recent FCC filings, with the M5 chip promising significant performance improvements over the M4.
