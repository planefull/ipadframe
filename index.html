Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but also create significant privacy risks due to "linkability," which allows separate uses of credentials to be tracked and associated with individuals. To combat this, Microsoft Research has introduced Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to common digital identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge proofs to enable users to selectively disclose information without revealing identifiable data or linking their interactions, all while working with existing infrastructure.

AirPods Pro 3 Won't Get 'Major' Audio or Active Noise Cancellation Improvements

Apple's upcoming AirPods Pro 3 are not expected to feature significant improvements in audio quality or Active Noise Cancellation, contrary to previous rumors. Instead, the new earbuds will introduce heart rate tracking, a health-related feature that uses LED optical sensors. Additionally, the AirPods Pro 3 will come with an updated design for a better fit and a slimmer charging case.
