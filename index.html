Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but introduce privacy risks through "linkability," where separate uses of electronic credentials can be tracked to create user profiles. To combat this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent leverages zero-knowledge proofs, allowing users to selectively disclose information without revealing identifiers that could link their separate digital interactions, all without requiring issuers to update their systems.
