RenderFormer: How neural networks are reshaping 3D rendering

Microsoft Research has developed RenderFormer, a novel neural architecture that revolutionizes 3D rendering by replacing traditional physics-based methods with a purely machine learning approach. This transformer-based model can perform full-featured 3D rendering, including arbitrary 3D geometry, material data, and complex global illumination, without relying on conventional graphics computations like ray tracing or rasterization. RenderFormer represents a significant step toward a general-purpose neural rendering model, offering greater generalizability and potential for integration into advanced AI systems for applications in gaming, film, and virtual reality.

iPhone 17 Pro vs. iPhone 17 Pro Max: What's Exclusive to Apple's Largest Flagship iPhone
