Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while offering convenience, create privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers in credentials like mobile driver's licenses and app logins allow for tracking and profiling of user behavior across different interactions. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that introduces "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats, including JSON Web Tokens. Crescent utilizes efficient zero-knowledge proofs to prevent separate digital activities from being linked, thereby enhancing privacy without requiring credential-issuing organizations to update their existing systems.
