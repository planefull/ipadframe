Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Diagnosing rare genetic diseases through whole genome sequencing is challenging for genetic professionals due to vast data, information overload, and difficulties in reanalyzing unsolved cases. To address this, Microsoft Research, Drexel University, and the Broad Institute developed and studied a generative AI assistant designed to support genetic professionals in synthesizing biomedical data. This AI-enhanced tool aims to streamline workflows by flagging cases for reanalysis and aggregating scientific literature, ultimately increasing diagnostic rates and improving patient outcomes for rare diseases.
