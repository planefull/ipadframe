Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Microsoft Research "Paraphrase Project" uncovered a "zero-day" vulnerability where AI-powered protein design tools could generate toxic protein variants capable of bypassing current biosecurity screening systems. A cross-sector team then utilized an AI red-teaming approach to develop and implement more robust screening "patches," sharing these updates globally with DNA synthesis companies to strengthen biosecurity defenses. This project also established a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" process for responsibly sharing sensitive scientific data, setting a precedent for managing information hazards in dual-use technologies.

Facebook's New Collage Feature May Use Your Images for AI Training

Meta is introducing an opt-in AI-based collage and photo editing tool for Facebook users in the U.S. and Canada, which scans camera rolls, uploads images to the cloud, and uses AI to create suggested edits. This feature generates private collages, recaps, or themed edits that users can choose to share with friends. Crucially, if users share any AI-created content, those specific images will then be used for Meta's AI training, raising privacy concerns and leading to advice for users to avoid the feature or restrict camera roll access.
