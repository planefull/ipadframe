Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, introduce significant privacy risks due to "linkability," allowing separate uses of credentials to be associated with a specific person and enable extensive tracking. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent achieves this using zero-knowledge proofs, enabling users to prove specific facts about their credentials without revealing any linking identifiers or requiring issuing organizations to update their systems.

Apple Preparing iOS 18.7 for iPhones as iOS 26 Release Date Nears

Apple is preparing to release iOS 18.7, anticipated in September, primarily to deliver security vulnerability fixes. This update will be one of the final ones for iPhone XS, XS Max, and XR, as these models are not compatible with the forthcoming iOS 26. iOS 26 is also nearing its public release around mid-September, with support for iPhone 11 and newer devices.
