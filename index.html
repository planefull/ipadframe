Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, pose significant privacy risks due to "linkability," where hidden identifiers allow separate uses of credentials to be tracked and user profiles to be built. To combat this, Microsoft Research has developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that introduces "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge SNARKs and a two-stage proof generation process, enabling users to prove specific facts about their credentials without revealing underlying data or linking separate interactions, and importantly, without requiring issuers to update their systems.
