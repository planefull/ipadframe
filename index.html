Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers can connect a user's various digital activities, enabling tracking and detailed profiling. To combat this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add *unlinkability* to widely used identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent leverages zero-knowledge proofs with a two-stage process to allow users to selectively present information without revealing linkable data, significantly enhancing privacy without requiring issuers to update their existing systems.

Apple Blocks iTorrent App From EU Alternative App Marketplace

Apple has revoked the distribution rights for the torrenting app iTorrent on AltStore PAL, an alternative app marketplace in the European Union. While the EU's Digital Markets Act typically provides more freedom for these marketplaces, Apple's Notarization process allows them to review apps for security and fraud. The iTorrent developer, however, claims Apple provided no specific reason for the revocation, and Apple's responses have been vague, leaving the cause of the ban uncertain.
