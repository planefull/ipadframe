Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project, led by Microsoft Chief Scientific Officer Eric Horvitz, revealed that AI tools could redesign toxic protein sequences to evade existing biosecurity screening systems, identifying a "zero-day" vulnerability in AI-powered protein design. This discovery prompted a cross-sector "red-teaming" effort to develop and globally share a more robust protein screening system, effectively patching the biosecurity gap. The project also established a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" approach for responsibly sharing sensitive research details, setting a new standard for managing information hazards in dual-use technology studies.
