Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities introduce privacy risks, particularly "linkability" which allows separate uses of digital credentials to be tracked and associated with a specific person. To counter this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that brings "unlinkability" to common digital identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent leverages zero-knowledge proofs and a two-stage process (prepare and show) to enable users to selectively disclose information from their credentials without revealing linking identifiers, all without requiring existing credential issuers to update their systems.

Apple Previews New Store With Unified Genius Bar and Pickup Counter

Apple has previewed its third retail location in India, Apple Hebbal, situated in Bengaluru's Phoenix Mall of Asia. This new 8,000-square-foot store is the first to feature a unified Genius Bar and Pickup counter, merging technical support and online order collection services. Staffed by 70 employees, Apple Hebbal officially opens to the public on September 2, joining its counterparts in Mumbai and Delhi.
