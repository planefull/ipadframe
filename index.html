Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project revealed a significant biosecurity vulnerability where AI tools could "paraphrase" toxic protein sequences, like ricin, allowing them to evade existing screening systems used by DNA synthesis companies. In response, a cross-sector team developed a cybersecurity-style defense, creating a more robust protein screening system and sharing these "patches" globally through organizations like the International Gene Synthesis Consortium. This project also pioneered a "perpetual gatekeeping" strategy for responsible data sharing, establishing a model for proactively addressing the dual-use dilemmas and information hazards posed by rapidly advancing AI and synthetic biology.

iPhone Air to Launch in China This Week

Apple CEO Tim Cook announced that the iPhone Air will be available for pre-order in China this Friday, October 17, with a launch set for October 22. Its release was initially postponed due to regulatory issues surrounding its eSIM-only design, a feature not traditionally supported by Chinese carriers for iPhones. However, all three state-owned telecommunications operators will now provide eSIM support, clearing the way for the 5.6mm thick device, which starts from 7,999 yuan.
