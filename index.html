Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but present privacy risks due to "linkability," which allows separate uses of credentials to be tracked and associated with a specific person. To counter this, Microsoft Research has developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent achieves this by using efficient zero-knowledge proofs, enabling users to selectively disclose information without revealing linking identifiers, all without requiring credential issuers to update their systems.

Adobe Premiere App Launching on iPhone and iPad Later This Month

Adobe is releasing a full-fledged version of its Premiere video editor for iPhone and iPad on September 30, available for pre-order now. This new app promises "pro-quality video editing" with features like a multi-track timeline, 4K HDR support, AI tools from Adobe Firefly, and direct social media exports. Free to use with optional paid services, it will replace the existing Premiere Rush app, bringing a more advanced editing experience to mobile devices.
