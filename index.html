Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The "Paraphrase Project" revealed a critical biosecurity vulnerability where AI tools could redesign toxic protein sequences to bypass existing screening systems, posing a "zero day" threat. A collaborative cross-sector team, including Microsoft, IBBIS, and Twist Bioscience, developed and implemented "patches" to fortify nucleic acid synthesis screening against these AI-generated threats. This groundbreaking effort not only enhanced global biosecurity but also established a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" strategy for responsibly sharing sensitive dual-use research data, setting a new standard for managing information hazards in AI-driven biology.

Everything New in iOS 26.1 Beta 2

Apple's iOS 26.1 and iPadOS 26.1 beta 2 introduces several new features and refinements, most notably a "slide to stop" option for alarms and timers, and a toggle for automatically downloading security improvements. iPadOS users will see the return of the Slide Over multitasking feature, and the Fitness app on iPhone now allows for custom workout creation. The update also includes numerous user interface tweaks, such as left-aligned text in the Settings app and Home Screen folders, along with subtle adjustments to the Liquid Glass design elements.
