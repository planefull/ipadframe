Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The "Paraphrase Project" by Microsoft Research, in collaboration with industry and non-profit partners, identified a critical "zero day" vulnerability where AI tools could redesign toxic proteins to bypass current biosecurity screening systems. Researchers developed and implemented more robust protein screening methods, sharing these "patches" with global DNA synthesis companies through a cybersecurity-style response. This effort not only strengthened biosecurity against AI-enabled threats but also established a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" strategy for responsibly sharing sensitive research data, setting a new standard for managing dual-use dilemmas in scientific disclosure.

iOS 26.1 Beta 4 Lets Users Control Liquid Glass Transparency with New Toggle

Apple has introduced a new setting in the latest betas of iOS 26.1, iPadOS 26.1, and macOS 26.1, allowing users to customize the appearance of "Liquid Glass." This new toggle lets users select between a more transparent "clear" look (the current design) or a "tinted" option that increases opacity and adds contrast. The customization applies system-wide, including apps and Lock Screen notifications, and can be found in Display and Brightness settings on mobile devices or Appearance settings on Mac.
