Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project uncovered a "zero-day" vulnerability where AI tools could redesign toxic proteins to evade existing biosecurity screening systems, prompting a two-year cross-sector effort. A team including Microsoft, Twist Bioscience, and IBBIS developed an AI "red-teaming" pipeline to demonstrate this risk and then engineered a more robust, AI-resilient protein screening solution, which was shared globally with synthesis companies. This collaboration also pioneered a "perpetual gatekeeping" strategy for responsible data disclosure, balancing open science with mitigating information hazards for dual-use technologies.
