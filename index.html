Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but present significant privacy risks through "linkability," allowing separate uses of credentials to be tracked and profiled. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that introduces "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge proofs to enable users to selectively disclose information and prevent linking between their digital interactions, all without requiring credential-issuing organizations to update their existing infrastructure.
