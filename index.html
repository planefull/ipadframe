iOS 26.2 Might Let iPhone Users in the EU Replace Siri With a Third-Party Assistant

Apple's iOS 26.2 beta 3 reveals groundwork for a future option allowing iPhone users in the EU to choose a default voice assistant other than Siri. The code indicates new Side Button behavior, enabling users to assign a third-party app to activate when the button is pressed and held, replacing Siri. This change is driven by the EU's Digital Markets Act, which mandates greater interoperability and user choice, and is expected to be limited to the European Union.
