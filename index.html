Breaking the networking wall in AI infrastructure 

AI system performance is currently bottlenecked by memory and network limitations, stemming from a fundamental trade-off between the short-range, power-efficient copper links and the long-range, power-hungry, less reliable optical fiber links. Microsoft Research introduces MOSAIC, a novel optical link technology that resolves this by employing a "wide-and-slow" architecture with hundreds of parallel low-speed microLED channels. This innovation promises to deliver copper-like power efficiency and reliability over long distances, enabling substantial power savings, reducing failure rates, and unlocking new, more scalable AI infrastructure designs that overcome the "networking wall."
