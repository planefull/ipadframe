Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but pose significant privacy risks due to "linkability," where hidden identifiers allow separate online activities to be tracked and associated with individuals. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent leverages efficient zero-knowledge proofs, allowing users to prove specific facts about their credentials without revealing linking data, thus protecting privacy without requiring issuing organizations to update their systems.
