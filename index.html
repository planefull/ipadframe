Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project discovered a "zero-day" vulnerability where open-source AI tools could redesign toxic protein sequences to evade existing biosecurity screening systems. A collaborative cross-sector team then developed and implemented "cybersecurity-style" patches to significantly strengthen these nucleic acid screening capabilities. This work not only enhanced biosecurity but also pioneered a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" strategy for responsibly sharing sensitive research, setting a new standard for managing information hazards in dual-use technologies.

Apple Introduces $2M Bug Bounty for Spyware-Level Exploits

Apple has significantly overhauled its bug bounty program, increasing the top reward to $2 million for sophisticated exploit chains that match mercenary spyware attacks, with total payouts potentially exceeding $5 million. The updated program emphasizes complete exploit chains, rather than individual vulnerabilities, and introduces "Target Flags" to enable immediate bounty verification and quicker payments for researchers. Effective November 2025, this initiative also expands rewards for specific vulnerability categories and claims to offer the largest payouts in the industry.
