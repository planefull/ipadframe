Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project revealed a "zero-day" vulnerability where AI tools could redesign toxic protein sequences, like ricin, to evade existing biosecurity screening systems used by DNA synthesis companies. A cross-sector team used an AI "red-teaming" pipeline to generate thousands of these evasive protein variants, subsequently developing and implementing "patches" to strengthen global biosecurity screening tools. This collaborative effort not only improved defenses but also established a novel "perpetual gatekeeping" approach for responsible data and software sharing in dual-use research, balancing open science with minimizing misuse risks.
