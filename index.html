Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but pose significant privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers can track and profile users across different online activities. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that introduces "unlinkability" to widely used digital identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent leverages zero-knowledge proofs to enable users to selectively disclose information and prove facts about their credentials without revealing any data that could link separate interactions, all without requiring updates from credential issuers.
