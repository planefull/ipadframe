Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers can track and profile users across different credential uses. To counter this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to common identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent achieves this by using zero-knowledge proofs, allowing users to selectively disclose information and prove facts about their credentials without revealing underlying data or connecting separate interactions, all while being compatible with existing issuer systems.

Apple Says 11-Inch MacBook Air and Two Other Macs Are Now Obsolete

Apple has added three Mac models, including the 11-inch MacBook Air (Early 2015) and two 2017 MacBook Pro variants, to its obsolete products list. A product becomes obsolete seven years after its distribution ends, making it generally ineligible for repairs from Apple or authorized service providers. Additionally, specific iPhone 8 Plus models were moved to the vintage list, which means repairs are still possible for up to two more years before they become obsolete.
