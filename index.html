Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Genetic professionals face significant challenges in diagnosing rare genetic diseases due to information overload, difficulties in collaboration, and complex reanalysis of whole genome sequencing data, leading to a low initial diagnosis rate. To address these issues, Microsoft Research, in partnership with Drexel University and the Broad Institute, developed a generative AI-based assistant. This AI tool is designed to enhance genetic professionals' workflows by synthesizing biomedical data, flagging cases for reanalysis based on new scientific findings, and aggregating crucial gene and variant information, thereby aiming to improve diagnostic yield and reduce diagnosis time for rare diseases.
