Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks through "linkability," allowing hidden identifiers to connect a user's separate credential uses and build comprehensive behavior profiles. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to common identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent employs zero-knowledge proofs, optimized for mobile devices, to enable users to selectively disclose information without revealing underlying data that could link their interactions, all without requiring credential issuers to update their existing systems.

Alogic Debuts $2,000 32-Inch 6K Display With Touch Support

Alogic has announced the Clarity 6K Touch, a 32-inch 6K display that the company states is the world's first 6K touchscreen compatible with the Mac. This innovative monitor features a 6016 x 3384 resolution, 10-point multitouch functionality with stylus support via a Mac driver, 90W power delivery for MacBooks, and a versatile fold stand. Priced at $2,000, the Clarity 6K Touch is slated for release in mid-October, aiming to provide an iPad-like touch experience for Mac users.
