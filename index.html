Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, introduce significant privacy risks due to "linkability," where separate uses of digital credentials can be tracked and associated with a specific person. To address this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent achieves this by using efficient zero-knowledge proofs, allowing users to selectively disclose information without revealing identifiers that could link their digital interactions, and importantly, it works without requiring issuing organizations to update their systems.

iOS 26: Fine-Tune Podcast Playback Speeds

With iOS 26, Apple's Podcasts app finally introduces fine-grained playback speed controls, aligning it with features long available in rival podcast platforms. Users can now adjust playback speed in 0.1x increments, ranging from 0.5x to 3x, offering much greater flexibility compared to previous fixed presets. This enhanced control caters to various listening needs, from slow for language learning to fast for skimming, and conveniently remembers preferred settings for each individual podcast.
