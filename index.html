Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks by allowing hidden identifiers to "link" separate uses of credentials, enabling user tracking. To counter this, Microsoft Research created Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent achieves this by using zero-knowledge proofs, allowing users to verify specific facts about their credentials without revealing underlying data or allowing their separate digital interactions to be connected, all without requiring credential issuers to update their systems.

All iPhone 17 and iPhone 17 Pro Battery Capacities Allegedly Leaked

Alleged battery capacities for Apple's upcoming iPhone 17 series, including the iPhone 17 Air, Pro, and Pro Max, have reportedly surfaced from a Chinese regulatory database. The leaked capacities indicate that models without a physical SIM card tray will generally feature slightly larger batteries compared to their SIM-slot counterparts. Notably, the iPhone 17 Pro Max is expected to be the first iPhone to cross the 5,000 mAh battery mark, while the ultra-thin iPhone 17 Air will have a smaller capacity around 3,000 mAh.
