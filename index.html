Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Diagnosing rare genetic diseases through whole genome sequencing is challenging for genetic professionals due to information overload, complex data synthesis, and inefficient reanalysis processes, resulting in low initial diagnostic rates. Microsoft Research, Drexel University, and the Broad Institute conducted a study to design a generative AI-based assistant to streamline these critical sensemaking tasks. This AI prototype aims to improve diagnostic yield and reduce diagnosis time by flagging cases for reanalysis and synthesizing vast amounts of scientific literature, fostering collaboration and enhancing patient outcomes.

iPhone Driver's Licenses Coming to North Dakota Residents

North Dakota residents will soon be able to add their driver's licenses and state IDs to the Apple Wallet app, allowing for digital verification at select U.S. airports and for age checks. This feature, which North Dakota DOT expects to launch within the next month for a $5 fee, expands Apple Wallet's digital ID support beyond the current states. While convenient, users should still carry their physical ID as the digital version is not universally accepted.
