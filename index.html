Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers can connect a user's separate online activities and build profiles. To combat this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used digital identity formats, including JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes optimized zero-knowledge proofs to allow users to selectively disclose information without revealing underlying data or linking different interactions, all without requiring credential issuers to update their systems.
