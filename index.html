Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, pose significant privacy risks due to "linkability," where hidden identifiers can track users' activities across different applications. Microsoft Research has developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses, preventing credentials from being linked to a specific person or activity. Crescent achieves this by using zero-knowledge proofs with a two-stage process for efficient mobile device use, allowing users to selectively disclose information without revealing identifying data or requiring system updates from credential issuers.

iPhone Lifts Smartphone Market Shipments as Rivals Stall

Apple's iPhone shipments are projected to grow by nearly 4% in 2025, becoming the main catalyst for a 1% increase in the global smartphone market, which would otherwise be stagnant or decline. This resilience is driven by strong customer loyalty, effective trade-in offers, and installment plans, contrasting with weaker Android sales. The overall industry is also seeing a focus on premiumization and rising average selling prices, with iPhone sales in the U.S. significantly influenced by major carriers and their promotional activities.
