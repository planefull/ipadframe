Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The Model Context Protocol (MCP) has enabled a "society of tools" for AI agents, allowing for unprecedented horizontal integration across various services and developers. However, this diversity introduces "tool-space interference," leading to reduced effectiveness due to challenges like an excessive number of tools, overly long responses, complex parameter schemas, and naming collisions. To mitigate these issues and foster a more reliable ecosystem, the article recommends implementing formal namespaces, enhancing resource sharing protocols, encouraging transparent server documentation, and developing client-side optimizations.
