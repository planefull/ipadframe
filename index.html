Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Genetic professionals face significant challenges in diagnosing rare diseases due to the vast and complex data from whole genome sequencing, leading to information overload, inefficient collaboration, and difficulties in prioritizing case reanalysis. A collaborative study by Microsoft Research, Drexel University, and the Broad Institute explored integrating generative AI to support these professionals in "sensemaking" tasks. They co-designed an AI assistant prototype that flags unsolved cases for reanalysis based on new scientific findings and synthesizes gene and variant information from literature, aiming to enhance diagnostic efficiency and improve patient outcomes.
