Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The article discusses the transition of agentic AI from vertically integrated systems to a "society of agents" leveraging heterogeneous tools via protocols like the Model Context Protocol (MCP). While MCP significantly expands agent capabilities through horizontal integration, it introduces "tool-space interference" due to challenges such as excessively large tool catalogs, lengthy responses, complex parameter schemas, naming collisions, and inadequate error messages. To mitigate these issues, the authors recommend implementing formal namespaces, improving client-provided resource specifications, encouraging detailed server documentation, and optimizing client-side tool management and marketplaces to foster a more reliable and interoperable AI ecosystem.

iOS 26's Liquid Glass Design Draws Criticism From Users

Two days after its release, Apple's new iOS 26 "Liquid Glass" design has proven highly divisive, garnering significant criticism on forums and social media. Users report complaints ranging from visual distraction, poor readability, and sluggish performance on older devices to an inconsistent or "cartoony" aesthetic, while a smaller group praises its modern, fun, and technologically impressive feel. The situation mirrors the controversial launch of iOS 7, suggesting Apple is likely to refine the design over time rather than reverse course, with options available for users to reduce some of its visual effects.
