Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The rise of diverse AI agents and the Model Context Protocol (MCP) ecosystem is leading to "tool-space interference," where heterogeneous tools hinder overall agent effectiveness. This interference stems from challenges like managing numerous tools, handling excessively long responses, dealing with complex parameters, resolving naming collisions, and interpreting poor error messages. To mitigate these issues, the article recommends formal namespaces, improved resource sharing protocols, transparent server documentation, and client-side optimizations for a more robust and scalable agent infrastructure.

iFixit Teardown Reveals How Apple Made the iPhone Air So Thin

iFixit's teardown of the new iPhone Air, Apple's thinnest iPhone to date, reveals how its 5.6mm frame was achieved through a camera "plateau" housing part of the logic board and a metal-encased battery, all within a robust titanium frame. Notable features include a 3D-printed USB-C port and multiple custom-designed Apple chips, including the C1X 5G modem. Despite its thin design, the iPhone Air surprisingly received a provisional repairability score of 7/10, thanks to easily removable components like a clipped-in display and back glass, battery adhesive loosened by electrical current, and Apple's reduced software restrictions on parts.
