Ideas: More AI-resilient biosecurity with the Paraphrase Project

The Paraphrase Project revealed a "zero-day" vulnerability where AI tools could redesign toxic proteins, like ricin, to evade existing biosecurity screening systems used by DNA synthesis companies. A cross-sector team then developed and deployed more robust screening tools and a novel perpetual gatekeeping strategy for sensitive research, effectively patching this vulnerability and enhancing global biosecurity. This pioneering effort underscores the dual-use nature of AI in biology, serving as a critical model for proactive defense, international collaboration, and responsible information sharing to ensure safe scientific advancement.

Apple's Smart Glasses With In-Lens Display May Feature Two Modes

Apple's upcoming second-generation smart glasses with an in-lens display are rumored to feature two distinct operating modes. These modes would adapt to the connected device, running a full visionOS when paired with a Mac and a lighter, mobile-friendly interface when connected to an iPhone. The first-generation glasses, expected by 2027, will lack an in-lens display but will include speakers, cameras, voice control, and potentially health features, competing with Meta Ray-Bans.
