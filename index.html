Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities offer convenience but introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers can connect separate credential uses to build detailed user profiles, even with existing selective disclosure features. To address this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that brings "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats, including JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge proofs, optimized for mobile devices, allowing users to prove facts about their credentials without revealing unnecessary data or linking their interactions, all without requiring issuers to update their systems.
