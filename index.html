Breaking the networking wall in AI infrastructure 

AI systems are currently hampered by network and memory bottlenecks caused by the trade-off between power-efficient, short-distance copper links and power-hungry, less reliable long-distance optical fiber links. To address this, Microsoft Research, Azure, and M365 developed MOSAIC, a novel optical link technology utilizing a "wide-and-slow" architecture with hundreds of parallel low-speed microLED channels. This approach offers the benefits of low power consumption, high reliability, and an extended reach of up to 50 meters, promising significant power savings and enabling more scalable and efficient AI infrastructure designs.
