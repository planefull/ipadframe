Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The article discusses the emergence of a "society of agents" leveraging the Model Context Protocol (MCP) for horizontal integration of AI tools, moving beyond vertically integrated systems. However, this proliferation introduces "tool-space interference," manifesting as reduced effectiveness due to issues like excessively large tool sets, lengthy responses, complex parameters, naming collisions, and inadequate error messages. To address these challenges, the authors propose recommendations for protocol developers to implement formal namespaces and enhance resource sharing, for server developers to provide transparent documentation, and for client and market developers to adopt optimization strategies and standardize best practices.

iOS 26 Includes Adaptive Temperature Option for Automatic Thermostat Adjustment

iOS 26 introduces a new Home app feature called Adaptive Temperature, designed to automatically adjust thermostats based on user presence and activity. This feature utilizes Activity History, location data, and optionally occupancy sensors to switch between "home," "away," and "extended away" states, predicting user arrival and optimizing temperature. However, some users are currently reporting difficulties finding or enabling the Adaptive Temperature setting on their HomeKit or Matter-compatible thermostats.
