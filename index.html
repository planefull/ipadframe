Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, introduce privacy risks like "linkability," where hidden identifiers in credentials can track and profile user activities across various applications. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent uses zero-knowledge proofs to ensure that users can selectively disclose information without revealing underlying data or allowing their separate digital interactions to be linked, enhancing privacy without requiring system updates from credential issuers.

iOS 26: 5 New Features in the Wallet App

iOS 26 brings significant enhancements to the Wallet app, including an expanded order tracking feature that utilizes Apple Intelligence to monitor all online purchases, not just those made with Apple Pay. Users can now add a U.S. digital passport for domestic travel at select TSA checkpoints and for identity verification online, alongside improved boarding passes with airport navigation, Find My integration, and Live Activities support. Other updates include the ability to store full credit card details for autofill and new controls to disable promotional offers within the app.
