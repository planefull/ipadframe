Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, introduce privacy risks like "linkability," enabling tracking of user activity across various applications and services. To address this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats such as JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses, without requiring updates to issuer systems. Crescent utilizes zero-knowledge SNARKs and a two-stage proof generation process to allow users to selectively disclose information while ensuring their separate digital interactions remain unlinkable, significantly enhancing privacy.
