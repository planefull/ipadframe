Tell me when: Building agents that can wait, monitor, and act

Modern LLM agents struggle with long-running monitoring tasks, such as watching emails or prices, because they lack the ability to know *when* to check, leading to either premature abandonment or excessive resource use. To address this, Microsoft Research introduced SentinelStep, a mechanism that wraps agents in a workflow with dynamic polling and careful context management, allowing them to patiently monitor conditions for hours or days. Implemented in their Magentic-UI prototype, SentinelStep significantly improves agent reliability for extended tasks, paving the way for more practical, proactive, and resource-efficient AI assistants.

iOS 26.1 Beta Liquid Glass Battery Drain Test: Tinted vs Clear Mode

This article details a test to determine if iOS 26.1's new "Tinted" Liquid Glass option or other transparency settings affect iPhone battery life. The author performed multiple controlled 2.5-hour tests on an iPhone 17 Pro Max, comparing various configurations of Liquid Glass and accessibility features. The results showed no meaningful difference in battery consumption across any of the tested settings, suggesting visual preference is the only reason to enable them.
