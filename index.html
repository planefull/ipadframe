Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Rare genetic disease diagnosis is challenging due to the vast and complex whole genome sequencing data, leading to low initial diagnosis rates and long waits for patients. To address this, Microsoft Research, Drexel University, and the Broad Institute collaboratively developed a generative AI-based assistant to streamline the "sensemaking" process for genetic professionals. This AI assistant is designed to improve diagnostic yield and reduce diagnosis time by flagging cases for reanalysis based on new scientific findings and efficiently synthesizing gene and variant information from scientific literature.
