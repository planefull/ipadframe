Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, create significant privacy risks through "linkability," allowing various uses of credentials like mobile driver's licenses or app logins to be tracked and profiled. To address this, Microsoft Research has introduced Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used digital identity formats. Crescent leverages zero-knowledge proofs, optimized for efficiency on mobile devices through preprocessing, to enable users to selectively disclose information without revealing identifying data or linking separate interactions, all without requiring updates to existing credential issuance systems.
