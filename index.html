RenderFormer: How neural networks are reshaping 3D rendering

Microsoft Research has developed RenderFormer, a novel neural architecture that revolutionizes 3D rendering by learning the entire graphics pipeline through machine learning, eliminating the need for traditional physics-based computations. This transformer-based model utilizes triangle tokens for scene geometry and material data, and ray bundle tokens for viewing direction, processed by dual transformers to handle both view-independent and view-dependent effects like global illumination. RenderFormer's ability to render complex 3D scenes with realistic lighting entirely with AI represents a significant leap forward, offering greater generalizability and paving the way for its integration into broader AI systems for diverse visual computing applications.
