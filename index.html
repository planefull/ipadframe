Tool-space interference in the MCP era: Designing for agent compatibility at scale

The article discusses "tool-space interference" as a new challenge arising from the proliferation of agentic AI and the Model Context Protocol (MCP), which enables a "society of tools" through horizontal integration. This integration, while extending capabilities, leads to issues such as large tool counts, excessively long responses, complex parameter schemas, naming conflicts, and poor error handling, which collectively reduce end-to-end effectiveness. To address this, the authors recommend implementing formal namespaces in MCP, enhancing client-provided resource sharing, encouraging transparent server documentation, and developing client-side optimizations to ensure better agent compatibility and a more robust ecosystem.

iFixit Teardown Reveals How Apple Made the iPhone Air So Thin

iFixit's teardown of the new iPhone Air, Apple's thinnest iPhone and a significant redesign, revealed innovative engineering solutions like a camera "plateau" that partially houses the logic board to fit components within its 5.6mm frame. Despite its slimness, the device is surprisingly repairable, featuring an easily removable display and back glass, along with a battery that uses adhesive loosened by electrical current. Overall, the iPhone Air earned a provisional repairability score of 7 out of 10, also showcasing Apple's custom C1X 5G modem and a 3D-printed USB-C port.
