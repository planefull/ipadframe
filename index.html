Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, introduce significant privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers can track and connect a user's various digital activities. To address this, Microsoft Research developed Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent utilizes efficient zero-knowledge proofs to allow users to selectively disclose information without revealing linking identifiers, thereby enhancing privacy without requiring issuing organizations to update their existing systems.

iOS 26: Fine-Tune Podcast Playback Speeds

With iOS 26, Apple's Podcasts app finally gains more fine-grained playback speed controls, catching up with rival applications. The update expands the speed range from 0.5x to 3x and introduces 0.1x incremental adjustments, moving beyond previous rigid presets. Users can adjust speeds via a new dial interface in the fullscreen playback controls, with the app also remembering custom settings for each individual podcast.
