Using AI to assist in rare disease diagnosis

Genetic professionals face significant challenges, including information overload and difficulties in prioritizing case reanalysis, which hinder accurate and timely diagnosis of rare genetic diseases from whole genome sequencing data. Microsoft Research, in collaboration with Drexel University and the Broad Institute, developed a generative AI assistant prototype to address these issues by streamlining "sensemaking" tasks. This AI tool is designed to flag unsolved cases for reanalysis based on new scientific findings and efficiently synthesize vast amounts of gene and variant information from scientific literature, ultimately aiming to increase diagnostic yield and improve patient outcomes.
