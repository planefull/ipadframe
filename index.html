Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, though convenient, create privacy risks through "linkability," allowing hidden identifiers to connect separate uses of credentials and build user profiles. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent achieves this privacy protection by leveraging efficient zero-knowledge SNARKs with a two-stage process, allowing users to prove specific facts about their credentials without revealing linkable data or requiring extensive infrastructure changes.
