Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, such as those in phone wallets and app logins, introduce privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers can connect separate credential uses to build detailed user profiles. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library that adds "unlinkability" to widely used identity formats like JSON Web Tokens and mobile driver's licenses. Crescent uses efficient zero-knowledge proofs, pre-processing complex calculations, to allow users to prove specific facts about their credentials without revealing linking data or requiring issuing organizations to update their systems.

ASUS ProArt 6K Review: $1,299 Gets You a 32-Inch 6K Display

The ASUS ProArt 6K is a new 32-inch 6K display priced at $1,299, offering a more affordable alternative to Apple's Studio Display and Pro Display XDR. While it matches the Pro Display XDR in size and resolution, features accurate colors, and provides more diverse port options, its design is less premium, and its HDR performance and lack of local dimming fall short of the XDR's professional capabilities. Overall, it's a good display worth considering for users seeking a larger screen than the Studio Display without the high cost of the Pro Display XDR.
