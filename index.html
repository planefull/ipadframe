Crescent library brings privacy to digital identity systems

Digital identities, while convenient, introduce privacy risks through "linkability," where hidden identifiers in credentials like mobile driver's licenses and app logins can connect separate activities to a single person, enabling tracking and surveillance. To address this, Microsoft Research has released Crescent, a cryptographic library designed to add "unlinkability" to widely used digital identity formats, including JSON Web Tokens. Crescent uses efficient zero-knowledge proofs, optimized for mobile devices, to allow users to selectively prove facts about their credentials without revealing linking data, thus preventing their digital interactions from being connected.
