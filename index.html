Tell me when: Building agents that can wait, monitor, and act

Modern LLM agents struggle with long-running monitoring tasks, such as waiting for an email response or price drop, because they lack the ability to intelligently determine *when* to check for updates, leading to inefficient resource use or task failure. To solve this, Microsoft Research developed SentinelStep, a mechanism that enables agents to complete these tasks by dynamically adjusting polling intervals and managing context to prevent overflow. Implemented in their Magentic-UI prototype, SentinelStep significantly improves agent reliability for tasks lasting hours, paving the way for more patient, proactive, and efficient AI assistants.

Apple Launched Its Big New Vision for TV 10 Years Ago Today

A decade ago, Apple fundamentally transformed its Apple TV platform by launching the Apple TV HD, Siri Remote, and tvOS, signaling a major strategic shift towards an app-centric future for television. This overhaul introduced a dedicated App Store for third-party developers, enabling universal search, and positioning the device as a casual gaming and app platform, powered by the A8 chip and an innovative touch- and voice-controlled Siri Remote. While some aspirations for a Siri-driven gaming ecosystem haven't fully materialized, this launch nevertheless established the core design and functionality that defines the Apple TV experience to this day.
